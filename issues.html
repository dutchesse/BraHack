BRA ANATOMY 1-0-1

Underwire

Grrr, our biggest arch enemy, they hold it all up and give the girls a nice shape but our girls deserve so much better than this wire thingy. A nice silicone solution that molds to the body would be a nice idea for starters.

THE CUPS

Cups hold our girls in place. They can come with seams, padded foam, stretchy fabric, lace. Share your favorite type of cup and why or perhaps you can  think of an improvement to the cup that is not yet available.

the Band

The band holds it all together and is believed what gives breast the most support. This might indeed be true but I am a true believer of another theory and suspect better support under and aside the breasts might be a good strategy, thats right ... a re-enginered underwire :)

the Bridge

The bridge separates the girls. I think the bridge makes an excellent location for a decorative interchangeable front closure, like a piece of jewelry you can slide in and out.

the wings

The wings, not sure what to say about the wings besides that they cause chicken wings in the back ... I'm thinking something like the new underwear at the seams to where it become invisible and does not pinch your extra flesh.

the straps

Too narrow, too wide, too long, too short ... and yet another fraying disaster sometimes. Shouldn't they stay in place once adjusted to your requirements.

the hooks

The arch enemy's sidekick ... I know we are supposed to wash our delicates in a separate lingerie bag but seriously ... "we ain't got time fo that!". We toss all the laundry in one pile and the same pile ends up in the washer resulting in mangled hooks and yet another ruined for life. Hack for a cire ladies

bits & bobs: rings

Have not had one break on me yet but the straps often get twisted in them and cause itch and discomfort. I know I know, first world problems but perhaps someone has a better solution

bits & bobs: sliders

Sliders are the bits that makes your bra strap adjustable. Unfortunately they never seem to stay in the adjusted location and always seem to 'slide' out of position. A re-engineered lockable slider might be one of many possible solutions.
